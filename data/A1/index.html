Before I start, something important to understand is that "learning to learn is a skill". and it is a skill that you can develop. This means that the more you practice learning, the better you will become at it. So, don't be discouraged if you find it difficult at first. Keep practicing and you will improve over time.

Please do watch this video on developing a growth mindset, it is very important to understand that you can learn anything if you put your mind to it.

1. Alphabets

The French alphabet has 26 letters, just like the English alphabet. However, the pronunciation of some letters is different. Here is a list of the French alphabet with their pronunciations:

  1. A - ah
  2. B - bay
  3. C - say
  4. D - day
  5. E - uh
  6. F - eff
  7. G - zhay
  8. H - ash
  9. I - ee
  10. J - zhee
  11. K - kah
  12. L - ell
  13. M - emm
  14. N - enn
  15. O - oh
  16. P - pay
  17. Q - koo
  18. R - air
  19. S - ess
  20. T - tay
  21. U - oo
  22. V - vay
  23. W - doo-blay-vay
  24. X - eeks
  25. Y - ee-grek
  26. Z - zed

My thoughts

- When I was learning french, I found out alphabets E, G, J, Q, R, U has different pronunciations than English. So you need to learn carefully
- Alphabet "R" can be tricky for English speakers, as it is pronounced from the back of the throat. It takes practice to get it right.
- The way we pronunce alphabets W, X, Y in french are not going to be the same we pronounce in the french sentences (More on that later part of the A1)
